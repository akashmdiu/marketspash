A-B Trust

A legal arrangement to divide assets into two trusts (A and B) for tax benefits.

Learn more →

A-Shares

Shares that have voting rights and receive dividends, typically owned by insiders.

Learn more →

AAA

The highest credit rating given by rating agencies, indicating the lowest risk of default.

Learn more →

Ability-to-Pay Taxation

A tax system that considers the taxpayer's ability to pay the tax.

Learn more →

Abnormal Return

The return on an investment that deviates from its expected outcome.

Learn more →

Absolute Return

The return that an asset or portfolio achieves over a certain period.

Learn more →

Accelerated Depreciation

A method that records higher depreciation cost in the early years of an asset's life.

Learn more →

Acceleration Clause

A contract provision that allows a lender to require a borrower to repay all or part of a loan immediately.

Learn more →

Account Balance

The amount of money in a financial repository, such as a checking or savings account, at any given moment.

Learn more →

Account in Trust

A financial account opened by one person for the benefit of another.

Learn more →

Account Number

A unique string of numbers assigned to a customer's account by a financial institution.

Learn more →

Account Statement

A periodic summary of account activity with beginning and ending monetary balances.

Learn more →

Accounting

The process of recording financial transactions pertaining to a business.

Learn more →

Accounting Conservatism

An accounting principle that requires greater verification for positive financial outcomes than negative ones.

Learn more →

Accounting Cycle

The process of recording, processing, summarizing, and reporting an organization's financial transactions.

Learn more →

Accounting Equation

A fundamental equation in double-entry accounting (Assets = Liabilities + Equity).

Learn more →

Accounting Information System (AIS)

A system designed to collect, store, and process financial and accounting data.

Learn more →

Accounting Rate of Return (ARR)

A financial metric used to measure the profitability and feasibility of a project.

Learn more →

Accounting Ratio

A financial metric used to assess a company's financial health by comparing various line items within their financial statements.

Learn more →

Accounts Payable (AP)

Money owed by a business to its suppliers shown as a liability on a company's balance sheet.

Learn more →

Accounts Payable Turnover Ratio

A short-term liquidity measure used to quantify the rate at which a company pays off its suppliers.

Learn more →

Accounts Receivable (AR)

Money owed to a business by its clients shown as an asset on a company's balance sheet.

Learn more →

Accounts Receivable Aging

A report categorizing a company's accounts receivable by the length of time an invoice has been outstanding.

Learn more →

Accounts Receivable Financing

A type of asset-financing arrangement where the company uses its receivables as collateral in a financing agreement.

Learn more →

Accretion

The growth of a company's earnings, assets, or an investment portfolio.

Learn more →

Accretion of Discount

The increase in the value of a discounted instrument as time passes and it matures.

Learn more →

Accrual Accounting

An accounting method that records revenues and expenses when they are incurred, regardless of when cash is exchanged.

Learn more →

Accruals

Revenues or expenses that have been incurred but not yet recorded in the accounts.

Learn more →

Accrued Expense

An expense that a business has incurred but has not yet paid.

Learn more →

Accrued Income

Revenue that has been earned but not yet received.

Learn more →

Accrued Interest

Interest that has been incurred but not yet paid.

Learn more →

Accrued Liability

A financial obligation a company incurs over time but has not yet paid.

Learn more →

Accrued Revenue

Revenue that a company has earned but has not yet billed to its customer.

Learn more →

Accumulated Depreciation

The total depreciation of an asset since its purchase.

Learn more →

Accumulated Other Comprehensive Income

The total of non-operating gains and losses that bypass the income statement.

Learn more →

Accumulation/Distribution Indicator (A/D)

A technical analysis tool that shows cumulative flow of money into and out of a security.

Learn more →

Acid-Test Ratio

A financial metric that assesses a company's short-term liquidity.

Learn more →

Acquisition Accounting

A set of guidelines to report assets, liabilities, non-controlling interest, and goodwill of the acquired entity.

Learn more →

Active Management

An investment strategy that relies on the expertise of portfolio managers to choose investments.

Activity-Based Budgeting (ABB)

A budget system that assigns costs to activities and services measured by the level of activity.
